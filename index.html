Research

My main line is the study of non-Hermitian Hamiltonians whose effective dynamics emulate measurement-induced topological phases. My doctoral thesis, “The Interplay of Topology and Symmetry in Driven Dissipative Systems,” combines mathematical modeling with numerical simulation, and connects with open quantum systems, electronic transport, and quantum dynamics.

Non-Hermitian topology & quantum measurement

Non-Hermitian Hamiltonians emulating measurement-induced topological phases; pseudospectra as a methodological tool.

Localization in non-Hermitian systems

Localization–delocalization transitions in non-Hermitian systems with nonlinear terms, Floquet driving, and disorder.

Chiral phonons & electron–phonon hybridization

Electronically tunable gap opening enabled by electron–chiral-phonon hybridization in helical chains; chiral phonons in graphene-based low-dimensional structures.

Background

Before my PhD, I completed the Licenciatura en Física at the Universidad Nacional Autónoma de México (UNAM, 2019–2025), graduating with highest honors for the B.Sc. thesis “Chiral Phonons in Graphene Structures.” As a research assistant in the group of Dr. José Eduardo Barrios Vargas (Faculty of Chemistry, UNAM, 2021–2025), I developed tight-binding and lattice-dynamics codes and identified C₃-symmetric configurations as natural platforms for phonon chirality.

Publications

One manuscript in preparation on electron–chiral-phonon hybridization in helical chains.
